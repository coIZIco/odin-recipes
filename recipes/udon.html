Udon Peanut Butter Noodles

Description

Udon is one of my favorite types of noodles. It is just so thick and chewy.

This recipe is prefect for udon since the sauce is nice and thick, it just sticks right to the noodles.

Ingredients

  • 1 package of dried udon
  • 0.5 cup chicken broth
  • 1.5 tbs minced fresh ginger root
  • 3 tbs soy sauce
  • 3 tbs peanut butter
  • 1.5 tbs honey
  • 2 tsp chili oil
  • 3 cloves minced garlic
  • 1 whole rotisserie chicken, meat removed
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 0.25 cup chopped green onions
  • 0.25 cup chopped peanuts
  • 0.25 cup chopped fresh cilantro

Steps

  1. Bring large pot with lightly salted water to a rolling boil. Drop the udon in a few noodles at a time and return to a boil. Cook uncovered, stirring occasionally, until the udon has cooked through, but is still firm to the bite, 10-12 minutes. Drain and return to the pot.
  2. While the noodles are cooking, whisk the chicken broth, ginger, soy sauce, peanut butter, honey, chili oil, and garlic in a saucepan over medium-high heat. Bring to a boil, whisking until the peanut butter has melted. Pour the sauce over the noodles. Add the chicken and red bell pepper; toss until the noodles are evenly coated in the sauce. Sprinkle with green onions, chopped peanuts, and cilantro to serve.
